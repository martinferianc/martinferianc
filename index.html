About

I am a penultimate year MEng student at Imperial College London studying Electronic and Information Engineering. My interests lie in sustainable engineering projects and machine learning and its hardware acceleration. My main skills lie in coding (C++, assembly, Verilog, Python, Matlab, Java and a bit of web-development), prototyping (FPGAs, IoT and embedded systems) and initial project business case planning and presenting.

Embedded Systems

I guess everyone has heard of IoT and its potentiall, it really does not need to be explained. That is why I am always excited to be involved in projects involving embedded Systems, especially related to healthacare and precise control.

FPGAs

Field-Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGAs) are just awesome. Not only you can program them to be your toy processor, they can be fast, very fast. Therefore, I am excited about acceleration of algorithms throug implementation on FPGAs.

Machine Learning/AI

Yeah, again, you are reading machine learning. But a different kind, than just recognising cats and dogs. Think bigger, how about self-assembling neural networks? *wink, wink*

Short Story

Where am I in my journey?

  • 2006-2015

    My Humble Beginning

    I was a student at Gymnázium Jura Hronca in Bratislava, Slovakia at first in its primary school programme and then joined the cult of International Baccalaureate (IB). During IB, all my education, except Slovak language and literature, started to be in English. I did my igh-level classes in Maths, Physics and English.

  • 2015-2019

    Wannabe Engineer

    After my high school I went to Imperial College London, UK and stared my degree in masters of Electronic and Information Engineering. My superpower is being able, given I have a tonne of transistors, to build a small scale CPU, make up instructions and their syntax and program it to control something. Cool right? I think that is better than Spider Man, but hope to be better than Iron Man one day.
